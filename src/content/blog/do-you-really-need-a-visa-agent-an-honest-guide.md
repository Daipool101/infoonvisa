Search "visa help" and you'll find hundreds of agents promising to "get your visa approved." Some are genuinely useful. Many charge a hefty fee for something you could do yourself in an afternoon. Here's an honest breakdown so you can decide — not a sales pitch.

## What a visa agent actually does

A legitimate agent typically helps with:

- **Filling and reviewing the application form**

- **Organising your documents** into the right format

- **Booking the appointment** and guiding you through submission

What an agent **cannot** do: guarantee approval, speed up the embassy's decision, or influence the officer. **Anyone promising "guaranteed approval" is a red flag** — no agent has that power, and it often signals a scam.

## When an agent is genuinely worth it

- Your case is **complex** — a previous refusal, a name change, unusual travel history

- You're applying for a **notoriously tricky visa** and want a second pair of eyes

- You simply **value the time saved** and can afford the fee

- **Language or accessibility barriers** make the official process hard for you

## When you're probably wasting money

- It's a **simple e-Visa or visa-on-arrival** — these are designed to be done yourself in minutes

- The agent charges a **large markup** on top of the official fee for basic form-filling

- You're comfortable reading an **official checklist** and following steps

For straightforward tourist visas, the honest truth is: **most people can do it themselves** and keep the agent's fee.

## How to decide in 60 seconds

1. **Check your route first.** Look up the exact requirements, documents and steps for your passport and destination — free. [Browse every country here](/countries).

2. **Read the official checklist** linked on your route page.

3. If it looks clear → **do it yourself.** If it feels overwhelming or your case is complex → an agent may be worth it.

## If you go it alone, get these right

Most self-applications fail on the same few things — not the form itself:

- [Proof of funds](/blog/proof-of-funds-for-tourist-visa) — presented the right way

- A clear travel purpose and itinerary

- Avoiding the [common reasons applications get rejected](/blog/why-visa-applications-get-rejected)

Get those right and a tourist visa is very often a do-it-yourself job.
