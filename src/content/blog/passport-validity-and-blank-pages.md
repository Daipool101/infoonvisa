It's the simplest requirement to meet and one of the most common reasons people get turned away — at the visa stage *or* at the airport check-in desk. Your passport itself has to qualify before your visa can. Here's the whole thing in plain terms.

## The 6-month rule

Many countries require your passport to be valid for **at least six months beyond** your travel dates. Some ask for validity beyond your **entry** date, others beyond your **departure** date — always assume the stricter version.

**Why six months?** It's a buffer. If your trip is extended, you fall ill, or travel is disrupted, the country wants assurance your passport won't expire while you're on its soil. Airlines enforce it too — they can be fined for carrying a passenger who'll be refused entry, so **check-in staff will deny boarding** if your passport is short, even if the country might technically have let you in.

## How to count it correctly

Take your **return date**, add six months, and compare to your passport's **expiry date**. If expiry is *before* that, you're at risk — renew before you travel.

> Example: you return **1 December**. Six months later is **1 June**. If your passport expires **before 1 June**, renew it now.

Not every country uses six months — some require three, some just validity for the length of stay. But **six months is the safe universal standard**: meet it and you satisfy almost everyone.

## Blank pages

Most countries need **1–2 completely blank visa pages** (the pages labelled "Visas", not the endorsement pages) for the entry stamp or visa sticker. Frequent travellers run out of pages before their passport expires — and a full passport gets you refused just like an expired one.

**Check both:** enough validity *and* enough blank pages.

## Passport condition matters too

A passport that's water-damaged, has a torn or detached page, a loose cover, or an unreadable photo can be rejected outright — by the embassy or at immigration. If yours is in rough shape, renew it before applying.

## When to renew — the timing

- **Renew if your passport is within ~8 months of expiry** and you have any trip planned — this clears the 6-month rule with margin.
- **Renew early in peak seasons**, when passport offices are backlogged.
- Renewing changes your passport number, so **renew *before* you apply for the visa** — the visa is tied to the passport number it's issued against.
- A rejected trip costs far more than a renewal fee — when in doubt, renew.

## Quick self-check before any application

- ✅ Passport valid **6+ months** beyond your return date
- ✅ **1–2 blank** visa pages available
- ✅ No damage (intact cover, pages, clear photo)
- ✅ Renewed **before** starting the visa application, not after
