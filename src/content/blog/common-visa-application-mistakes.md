There's a difference between being *refused* and being *rejected on a technicality*. [Refusals](/blog/why-visa-applications-get-rejected) are about substance — funds, purpose, ties. But a huge number of applications never even get that far: they're bounced for **avoidable process mistakes**. Here are the 11 that come up most, and how to dodge each.

## 1. Using a generic checklist instead of the official one

Requirements differ by passport *and* destination. A forum list for "Schengen" won't match what your specific consulate wants.
**Avoid it:** work from the official checklist — every InfoOnVisa route page links the government source for your exact corridor.

## 2. Photo specification errors

Wrong size, wrong background colour, glasses, a smile where a neutral expression is required, or an old photo — all common auto-rejects.
**Avoid it:** follow the destination's exact photo spec (dimensions, background, recency). When unsure, use a photo service that knows the country's rules.

## 3. Typos and mismatches on the form

A misspelled name, a transposed passport number, or dates that don't match your bookings. Officers cross-check everything.
**Avoid it:** fill the form against your passport character by character; make dates match your itinerary and flights exactly.

## 4. Passport too close to expiry

Many countries need **6 months' validity** beyond travel and 1–2 blank pages.
**Avoid it:** check early — see [the 6-month passport rule](/blog/passport-validity-and-blank-pages) — and renew before applying if you're close.

## 5. Missing translations

Documents not in the destination's accepted language (often English or the local language) may need certified translations.
**Avoid it:** check which documents need translating and whether they must be certified/notarised.

## 6. The "show money" deposit

A large sum dumped into your account days before applying screams borrowed funds.
**Avoid it:** keep 3–6 months of clean statements; explain any genuine large deposit with evidence. Full detail in [proof of funds](/blog/proof-of-funds-for-tourist-visa).

## 7. No clear itinerary or purpose

Vague plans read as a cover story.
**Avoid it:** attach a simple day-by-day itinerary plus accommodation and flight reservations.

## 8. Non-refundable bookings bought too early

Buying real tickets before approval, then losing money if refused — or worse, feeling pressured to travel on a visa that isn't ready.
**Avoid it:** use refundable reservations for the application; see [flight & hotel bookings for a visa](/blog/flight-and-hotel-bookings-for-a-visa).

## 9. Applying to the wrong consulate

For multi-country trips (Schengen especially), applying anywhere other than your main destination is a refusal ground.
**Avoid it:** apply to the country of your longest stay, or first entry if evenly split.

## 10. Hiding a previous refusal

Forms ask directly, and records are shared. Concealing it turns a fixable issue into a misrepresentation finding with long bans.
**Avoid it:** disclose it and explain briefly. Honesty is always the safer bet.

## 11. Leaving it to the last minute

The appointment wait plus processing can be weeks — miss it and your trip is at risk.
**Avoid it:** follow a proper timeline — see [how early to apply](/blog/how-early-to-apply-for-a-visa).

## The 60-second pre-submit checklist

- ✅ Names/numbers/dates match your passport and bookings exactly
- ✅ Photo meets the country's spec
- ✅ Passport valid 6+ months, blank pages available
- ✅ 3–6 months of clean bank statements
- ✅ Itinerary + accommodation + flight reservations attached
- ✅ Insurance meeting the required minimum (if mandatory)
- ✅ Translations done where needed
- ✅ Correct consulate for your itinerary
- ✅ Previous refusals disclosed
- ✅ Submitting with weeks to spare
