Here's the catch-22 every applicant hits: the embassy wants to see your flights and hotels — but you don't want to buy non-refundable travel for a visa that hasn't been approved yet. Buy too early and a refusal costs you real money; skip it and your application looks incomplete. Here's how to handle it sensibly.

## What embassies actually want to see

For a tourist visa, consulates typically ask for:

- A **round-trip flight itinerary** showing you'll arrive and leave within your stated dates
- **Accommodation** for the duration (hotel bookings, or a host's address with an [invitation letter](/blog/invitation-and-sponsorship-letters))
- A coherent **day-by-day itinerary** tying it together

Crucially, most embassies want **proof of intended travel — not proof of purchase.** They are not asking you to gamble thousands on tickets before they've decided.

## Flight reservation vs. paid ticket

- A **flight reservation (itinerary/PNR)** is a held booking showing route, dates and passenger name — *not fully paid*. This is what you submit.
- A **paid ticket** is the real, purchased fare. You buy this **after** approval.

Ways to get a reservation without buying:
- **Refundable/flexible fares** you can cancel within the window
- Airline **"hold my fare"** options (many airlines hold a fare 24–72 hours, some longer for a small fee)
- A **reputable flight-reservation service** that issues a verifiable itinerary for visa use

⚠️ **A word of caution:** avoid shady "dummy ticket" sellers that produce fake PNRs. Embassies can and do verify bookings; a fake reservation is misrepresentation and can get you banned. Use a **verifiable** reservation, not a forged one.

## Accommodation — same principle

- Use **free-cancellation hotel bookings** for the whole stay (widely available on major booking sites) — real, verifiable, and cancellable if refused.
- Staying with family/friends? Provide their address plus an [invitation letter](/blog/invitation-and-sponsorship-letters) instead of hotels.
- Make the accommodation dates line up exactly with your flight dates and itinerary.

## The smart sequence

1. **Prepare** refundable flight reservation + free-cancellation hotels matching your dates
2. **Submit** the visa application with those
3. **Wait** for approval
4. **Then buy** the real tickets and lock in hotels
5. If refused, **cancel** the refundable bookings — minimal or zero loss

This is also why you should [apply with weeks to spare](/blog/how-early-to-apply-for-a-visa): you need time to get approval before real fares rise or availability shrinks.

## Common mistakes

- **Buying non-refundable flights before approval** — the expensive classic
- **Fake/dummy tickets from dubious sellers** — a misrepresentation risk
- **Booking dates that don't match** your form or itinerary
- **No accommodation for part of the stay** — gaps look suspicious
- **Cancelling the reservation before the decision** — some embassies re-check; keep it live until you're approved
