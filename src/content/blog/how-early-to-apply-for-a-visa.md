The most avoidable travel disaster is a visa that arrives *after* your flight. It happens constantly — not because the visa was refused, but because the applicant left it too late. Here's how visa timing actually works, and a safe schedule to follow.

## Two clocks you need to understand

Most travellers only think about **processing time** — how long the embassy takes to decide once you submit. But there are really two clocks:

1. **Appointment wait** — at busy consulates you may wait days or weeks just to get a slot to *submit*.
2. **Processing time** — how long the decision takes after submission.

Your real lead time is **both added together** — and both stretch in peak season.

## Rough processing times by visa type

These are typical ranges — always check the exact figure on your route page, which shows the official processing time for your specific corridor:

- **Visa-free / VOA:** none in advance — decided on arrival
- **e-Visa / ETA:** often 24 hours to a week; a few are near-instant, some take longer
- **Embassy / sticker visa (e.g. [Schengen](/blog/schengen-visa-explained), UK, US):** commonly 2–3 weeks for a decision — *plus* the appointment wait

## Why it takes longer than the "official" number

- **Peak season** (summer, Christmas, and major local festival periods) — application volumes spike and queues balloon.
- **Public holidays** in *both* countries pause processing — don't forget the destination's holidays.
- **Administrative processing** — some applications get pulled for extra checks, adding unpredictable weeks. Nationality and travel history influence this.
- **Document requests** — if the embassy asks for one more paper, the clock effectively restarts.

## A safe timeline

| When | Do this |
|------|---------|
| **2–3 months before** | Check your route's requirements; renew your [passport](/blog/passport-validity-and-blank-pages) if it's within ~8 months of expiry |
| **6–8 weeks before** | Book your consulate appointment (slots vanish fast in peak season) |
| **4–6 weeks before** | Gather documents, [proof of funds](/blog/proof-of-funds-for-tourist-visa), insurance, bookings |
| **3–4 weeks before** | Submit — this is the sweet spot for most embassy visas |
| **e-Visa routes** | Still apply **1–2 weeks ahead**, never the night before |

## How early is *too* early?

You usually can't apply whenever you like — most tourist visas accept applications only within a **window before travel** (Schengen, for example, opens at 6 months). Applying outside the window gets you turned away. So the rule is: **as early as the window allows, but not before it opens.**

## Smart moves to protect your dates

- **Don't buy non-refundable flights before your visa is approved.** Use refundable reservations for the application (see [flight & hotel bookings for a visa](/blog/flight-and-hotel-bookings-for-a-visa)).
- **Build in a buffer** — aim to have the visa in hand at least a week before departure.
- **Track the appointment calendar early** — the appointment wait, not the decision, is what catches most people out.
- If you travel often, ask whether you qualify for a **long-validity multiple-entry visa** next time — it removes this stress for years.
