Planning a trip should be exciting — but the visa step turns it into a maze of government PDFs, contradictory forum posts and outdated blogs. Here’s a clean way to get a trustworthy answer fast.

## 1. Start with your passport, not your destination

Visa rules depend on **the passport you hold**, not just where you’re going. The same destination can be visa-free for one nationality and embassy-only for another. Always frame it as *From [your country] → To [destination]*.

## 2. Identify the requirement type

Most routes fall into one of these buckets:

- **Visa-free** — just show up with a valid passport.
- **Visa on arrival (VOA)** — get it at the airport on landing.
- **e-Visa / ETA** — apply online before you fly.
- **Embassy visa** — apply in person or via a visa center (VFS/BLS).

Knowing which bucket you’re in tells you how much lead time you need.

## 3. Check the non-negotiables

Even visa-free travel has rules. The big three:

1. **Passport validity** — often 6 months beyond your travel dates.
2. **Onward/return ticket** — frequently asked at immigration.
3. **Proof of funds / accommodation** — for longer or scrutinised trips.

## 4. Use the official source — and note the date

Rules change. Whatever you read, confirm it on the official government or e-visa portal, and note when you checked. That “last verified” habit is exactly why InfoOnVisa dates every page.

## 5. Wrap the trip, not just the visa

Once the visa is sorted, line up travel insurance, an eSIM for arrival, and your flights. Doing it in one sitting saves the scramble later.
